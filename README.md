# EIUSC 2025
## 📊 OPTIMIZING SUPPLY CHAIN DECISIONS THROUGH COMPARATIVE DEMAND FORECASTING MODELS

Research Project: Demand Forecasting in Donor-Funded Pharmaceutical Supply Chains

This study benchmarks three forecasting models — SARIMA, Random Forest, 
and LSTM — to predict monthly demand for ARV and HIV-related commodities 
using 10,324 real-world shipment records.

Models are evaluated using MAE, RMSE, and WMAPE to compare statistical, 
machine learning, and deep learning approaches.

Results indicate that Random Forest achieves the lowest forecasting error, 
while SARIMA performs well on stable seasonal patterns and LSTM captures 
complex temporal dependencies.

The findings support improved inventory planning, shipment scheduling, 
and logistics cost optimization in global health supply chains.
